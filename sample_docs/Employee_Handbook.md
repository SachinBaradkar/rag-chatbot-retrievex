# Acme Corp — Employee Handbook
**Version 3.2 | Effective January 2024**

---

## 1. Leave Policy

### 1.1 Annual Leave
All full-time employees are entitled to **21 days** of paid annual leave per calendar year.
Leave must be applied for at least **5 working days** in advance via the HR portal.
Unused leave may be carried forward up to a maximum of **10 days** to the next year.

### 1.2 Sick Leave
Employees are entitled to **10 days** of paid sick leave per year.
A medical certificate is required for sick leave exceeding **3 consecutive days**.

### 1.3 Maternity & Paternity Leave
- Maternity leave: **26 weeks** (paid at full salary for the first 12 weeks)
- Paternity leave: **5 working days** (fully paid)

---

## 2. Code of Conduct

All employees must:
- Maintain professionalism and respect in all interactions.
- Protect confidential company information.
- Declare conflicts of interest to their manager immediately.
- Not engage in harassment, discrimination, or bullying.

Violations may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination.

---

## 3. Remote Work Policy

Employees may work remotely up to **3 days per week** subject to manager approval.
Core hours are **10:00 AM – 4:00 PM** local time regardless of location.
Employees are responsible for maintaining a secure, stable internet connection.

---

## 4. Performance Reviews

Performance reviews occur **twice per year** — in June and December.
Ratings: Exceeds Expectations | Meets Expectations | Needs Improvement | Unsatisfactory.
Employees rated "Needs Improvement" for two consecutive reviews may enter a Performance Improvement Plan (PIP).

---

## 5. Compensation & Benefits

### 5.1 Salary Review
Salary reviews take place annually in January. Merit increases average **4–7%** based on performance.

### 5.2 Health Insurance
The company covers **80%** of health insurance premiums for the employee and **50%** for dependents.

### 5.3 Retirement / Provident Fund
The company contributes **12%** of basic salary to the employee's provident fund account.

---

## 6. Grievance Procedure

Employees with complaints should:
1. Discuss the issue with their direct manager.
2. If unresolved, escalate to HR within **14 days**.
3. HR will acknowledge the grievance within **5 working days** and resolve within **30 days**.

---

## 7. Termination Policy

### 7.1 Notice Period
- Employees with less than 1 year of service: **1 month** notice.
- Employees with 1–5 years of service: **2 months** notice.
- Employees with more than 5 years of service: **3 months** notice.

### 7.2 Immediate Termination
The company reserves the right to terminate employment without notice for gross misconduct, including theft, fraud, or serious policy violations.

---

## 8. IT & Data Security

- Employees must use company-provided devices for business activities.
- Personal use of company equipment is limited and must not violate policy.
- All data must be stored on approved cloud storage (SharePoint or OneDrive).
- Employees must complete annual cybersecurity training.
